Design Principles

Bad Web Design - Mosaic

The Mosaic website suffers from outdated design and usability issues, making it challenging for users to navigate and accomplish tasks efficiently.

  • Consistency: The student center’s text-heavy links and misaligned sections clash with the polished login page.
  • Ease of Navigation: The student center’s text-heavy links and misaligned sections clash with the polished login page.
  • Universal Usability: The layout is not mobile-friendly and offers limited accessibility features for screen readers.
  • Error Prevention: Forms lack inline validation, and there are no safeguards like confirmation dialogs for sensitive actions.

Bad Web Design - YouTube

The YouTube interface demonstrates an excellent example of modern and user-friendly web design, focusing on clarity, accessibility, and efficiency. Its layout is visually appealing and structured in a way that makes it easy for users to explore content and perform actions with minimal effort. The interface is consistent across all pages, ensuring a smooth and intuitive experience for both new and returning users.

  • Consistency: The design uses uniform navigation icons, fonts, and a grid-based layout, making the interface predictable and easy to navigate. Ease of Navigation: Key sections like "Home," "Shorts," and "Subscriptions" are prominently displayed, with a central search bar and intuitive placement of shortcuts for quick access.
  • Ease of Navigation: Key sections like "Home," "Shorts," and "Subscriptions" are prominently displayed, with a central search bar and intuitive placement of shortcuts for quick access.
  • Universal Usability: The responsive design adapts seamlessly across devices, offering high contrast, clear clickable areas, and accessibility features for a diverse user base.
  • Error Prevention: Visual feedback, such as highlights on hover, intuitive categorization, and clear labeling, helps users avoid confusion and errors while navigating the platform.
